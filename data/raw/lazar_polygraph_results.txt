POLYGRAPH EXAMINATION REPORT
Subject: Robert Scott Lazar
Date of Examination: December 1989
Conducted by: Terry Tavernetti, Certified Polygraph Examiner
Second examination conducted by: Ron Slay, Polygraph Examiner

EXAMINATION CONTEXT:
Following Robert Lazar's public claims regarding employment at a classified facility near Area 51 designated "S4," polygraph examinations were requested to assess the veracity of his statements. Two separate examinations were conducted by independent examiners.

EXAMINATION BY TERRY TAVERNETTI:
Questions addressed:
1. Did you work at a facility called S4 near Area 51? (Response: Yes)
2. Did you observe technology of non-human origin? (Response: Yes)
3. Were you employed as a physicist at this facility? (Response: Yes)
4. Did you personally interact with Element 115? (Response: Yes)

Examiner's Conclusion:
"In my professional opinion, Mr. Lazar was being truthful when he answered the above questions. The polygraph charts showed no indications of deception. His physiological responses were consistent with someone who genuinely believes they experienced the events described."

Note: Tavernetti observed some signs of stress during questioning about government threats, which he attributed to genuine anxiety about repercussions rather than deception.

EXAMINATION BY RON SLAY:
The second examination by Ron Slay produced results that were described as "inconclusive" on some questions, while other questions indicated truthfulness. Slay noted that Lazar exhibited unusual physiological patterns that made definitive interpretation difficult.

Slay's statement: "The subject shows truthful responses on the core questions about his presence at a classified facility and his work on propulsion systems. However, some questions regarding specific technical details produced responses that I cannot categorize definitively as either truthful or deceptive. This may indicate stress, uncertainty about specific details over time, or the limits of the polygraph methodology itself."

IMPORTANT CAVEATS:
1. Polygraph examinations are not admissible in most courts of law and their scientific reliability is debated within the scientific community. The American Psychological Association has noted that "most psychologists agree that there is little evidence that polygraph tests can accurately detect lies."

2. A polygraph measures physiological stress responses (heart rate, blood pressure, galvanic skin response, respiration). It does not directly detect truth or falsehood. A subject who genuinely believes a false memory would register as "truthful."

3. These examinations were privately arranged and were not conducted under government or law enforcement protocols.

EXAMINER CREDENTIALS:
