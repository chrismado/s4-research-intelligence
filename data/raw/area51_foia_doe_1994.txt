SUBJECT: Nevada Test Site — Facility Operations and Environmental Monitoring
Classification: FORMERLY RESTRICTED DATA — DECLASSIFIED

SECTION 1: FACILITY OVERVIEW

The Nevada Test Site (NTS), encompassing approximately 1,360 square miles of desert terrain in Nye County, Nevada, has been used by the Department of Energy and its predecessor agencies (the Atomic Energy Commission and the Energy Research and Development Administration) for nuclear weapons testing since January 1951.

Adjacent to the NTS, the area commonly referred to as "Area 51" or "Groom Lake" is administered by the United States Air Force and falls under restricted airspace designation R-4808N. The Department of Energy acknowledges the existence of operations at the Groom Lake facility but does not administer or have oversight of Air Force programs conducted at that location.

SECTION 2: ENVIRONMENTAL MONITORING DATA

Environmental monitoring conducted at the NTS boundary stations from 1985 through 1993 has documented radiological measurements consistent with legacy nuclear testing programs. No anomalous radiological signatures were detected at monitoring stations south of Groom Lake in the Papoose Mountain range area during this period.

SECTION 3: PERSONNEL RECORDS

The Department of Energy maintains employment records for civilian contractors and government employees assigned to NTS operations. Under FOIA request DOE-1994-0347, the following has been determined:

- Employment records for the period 1985-1990 at NTS facilities are maintained in the DOE archives.
- Certain personnel assignments within the NTS complex are classified under separate compartmented programs and are not subject to FOIA disclosure under Exemption (b)(1) — classified information pertaining to national defense.
- Contractor employment at facilities adjacent to but not administered by DOE (including the Groom Lake installation) falls outside DOE record-keeping authority.

SECTION 4: FACILITY DESIGNATIONS

The NTS complex includes numbered areas (Area 1 through Area 30) designated for specific testing and operational purposes. These area designations are internal administrative references.

The DOE is not aware of a facility designated "S4" within its administrative jurisdiction at the Nevada Test Site. Area designations beyond the NTS boundary, including any facilities that may exist at or near Papoose Lake, fall under the jurisdiction of the Department of Defense and the United States Air Force.

Note: Requests for information regarding Air Force operations at or near Groom Lake should be directed to the Air Force Office of Special Investigations (AFOSI) or submitted as a separate FOIA request to the Department of Defense.
